How to study this track

A practical rhythm so the lessons compound instead of feeling like disconnected theory.

  • Start with PyTorch, optimization, and training dynamics before diving into specific architectures. You will understand every later model much faster.
  • Alternate between math-heavy lessons and implementation lessons. Attention, positional encodings, dropout, and schedulers land better when you can connect formulas to code.
  • Use CNN/RNN/GAN/autoencoder lessons as architecture literacy. Even if you work mostly with LLMs, these patterns sharpen your understanding of representation learning.
  • Keep robustness in view: interpretability, adversarial training, and efficient inference are what separate model enthusiasm from engineering maturity.

What this track gives you

Use this page as the high-level map, then open the highlighted lessons when you want the full breakdown, code, and exercises inside the academy’s lesson engine.

Training loops and optimization

Master AMP, gradient clipping, schedulers, regularization, and debugging signals like exploding loss or unstable gradients.

Architecture intuition

Transformers, CNNs, RNNs, GNNs, MoE, ViT, and U-Net become easier to reason about when you understand how information flows through them.

Compression and efficiency

Quantization, distillation, sparse routing, and transfer learning show how to make powerful models practical on real hardware and budgets.

Robustness and interpretability

Grad-CAM, SHAP, adversarial training, and self-supervised learning build the judgment needed for models that perform under pressure.
