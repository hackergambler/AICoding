How to study this track

A practical rhythm so the lessons compound instead of feeling like disconnected theory.

  • Build around pipelines, not isolated models. This track is strongest when you think in terms of data flow, preprocessing, training, validation, and monitoring as one system.
  • Do evaluation lessons early and revisit them constantly. Accuracy alone is never enough once your datasets become messy or imbalanced.
  • Use the classical algorithms as pattern recognition practice: what kind of signal structure makes trees, boosting, linear models, clustering, or anomaly detection the right tool?
  • Pair deployment and drift lessons with your own experiments so the jump from notebook metrics to real-world failure modes feels natural.

What this track gives you

Use this page as the high-level map, then open the highlighted lessons when you want the full breakdown, code, and exercises inside the academy’s lesson engine.

Pipelines and trustworthy data

Start with reproducible preprocessing, validation, and split discipline so every later experiment rests on clean assumptions.

Model quality beyond a leaderboard

Cross-validation, tuning, feature importance, and class imbalance help you understand whether a model is really learning or simply flattering a metric.

Algorithmic breadth

From linear models and trees to clustering, forecasting, recommendation, and anomaly detection, this track gives you enough range to choose the right family for the job.

MLOps thinking

Tracking, deployment, and drift detection make the track useful beyond notebooks by teaching how models survive contact with changing data and users.
